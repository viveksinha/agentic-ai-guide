⚡ Build Your First Agent

A step-by-step guide to building a Fidelity Annuity Advisor agent. Two paths: zero code in 5 minutes, or a full custom app with Python.

The Use Case: Annuity Intake & Recommendation
This is a real problem worth solving. Here's exactly what the agent replaces.

❌ Without Agent (Today)

  • Customer calls or emails Fidelity
  • Rep manually asks 4–6 follow-up questions
  • Rep looks up matching products
  • Rep estimates rough income figures
  • Rep schedules advisor call
  • Total: 20–30 minutes per inquiry

✅ With Agent

  • Customer types question in chat
  • Agent asks 4 clarifying questions
  • Agent recommends the right product
  • Agent offers to book advisor call
  • Agent emails summary to customer
  • Total: 3–5 minutes, no rep needed
ℹ️

Fidelity's annuity products: Fixed Annuity (guaranteed returns, 3–10 year horizon), Deferred Income Annuity (guaranteed lifetime income, $10K min), Variable Annuity (market-linked growth, 60+ fund choices). The agent recommends among these based on the customer's answers.

  3. 3

    Paste the Instructions

    Click "Project Instructions" and paste the prompt below. This is the agent's "brain" — it's all plain English.

Agent Instructions (Plain English — paste into Claude Project) 
You are an annuity advisor assistant for Fidelity Insurance.
Your job is to help customers figure out which annuity product fits their needs.

Ask the customer these questions one at a time (don't ask all at once):
1. How old are they?
2. When do they plan to retire (years from now)?
3. How much do they have to invest?
4. Do they want guaranteed fixed income, or are they okay with some market risk?

Based on their answers, recommend one of these Fidelity products:

- Fixed Annuity: guaranteed returns, low risk, 3-10 year horizon, minimum $5,000
- Deferred Income Annuity: guaranteed lifetime income starting 2+ years from now, minimum $10,000
- Variable Annuity: market-linked growth with 60+ fund choices, for people comfortable with some risk, minimum $10,000

After your recommendation, always end with:
"Would you like me to schedule a call with a Fidelity advisor to get exact numbers?"

Be warm, simple, and avoid financial jargon. Keep responses short — 2-4 sentences max.
Never give specific dollar amounts for future income — say "a Fidelity advisor can calculate your exact figures."

3. Create the Agent File

Create a file called annuity_agent.py and paste the full code below:

python — annuity_agent.py 
import anthropic

client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key="your-api-key-here")

SYSTEM_PROMPT = """
You are an annuity advisor assistant for Fidelity Insurance.
Your job is to help customers figure out which annuity product fits their needs.

Ask the customer these questions one at a time (don't ask all at once):
1. How old are they?
2. When do they plan to retire (years from now)?
3. How much do they have to invest?
4. Do they want guaranteed fixed income, or are they okay with some market risk?

Based on their answers, recommend one of these Fidelity products:
- Fixed Annuity: for people who want guaranteed returns, low risk, 3-10 year horizon
- Deferred Income Annuity: for people who want guaranteed lifetime income starting in 2+ years
- Variable Annuity: for people okay with market exposure for higher potential growth

Always end with: "Would you like me to schedule a call with a Fidelity advisor?"
Be warm, simple, and avoid financial jargon. Keep responses short.
"""

def run_annuity_agent():
    print("Fidelity Annuity Assistant")
    print("-" * 40)
    print("Agent: Hi! I can help you find the right annuity. What's on your mind?\n")

    conversation_history = []

    while True:
        user_input = input("You: ").strip()
        if user_input.lower() in ["exit", "quit", "bye"]:
            print("Agent: Thanks for chatting! Have a great day.")
            break

        # Add user message to history
        conversation_history.append({
            "role": "user",
            "content": user_input
        })

        # Send to Claude
        response = client.messages.create(
            model="claude-opus-4-5",
            max_tokens=500,
            system=SYSTEM_PROMPT,
            messages=conversation_history
        )

        agent_reply = response.content[0].text

        # Add agent reply to history so it remembers the conversation
        conversation_history.append({
            "role": "assistant",
            "content": agent_reply
        })

        print(f"\nAgent: {agent_reply}\n")

run_annuity_agent()
bash — Run It 
python annuity_agent.py
Adapt This to Any Insurance Workflow
The only thing you need to change is the instructions. The code stays exactly the same.
Change the instructions to...And the agent becomes...
Describe your claims intake questions and routing rules A claims FNOL (First Notice of Loss) agent
List your underwriting appetite and eligibility rules An underwriting pre-screening agent
Describe your policy renewal workflow and messaging A renewal outreach and processing agent
List your broker products and commission structure A broker quote and support assistant
Describe your fraud indicators and red flags A claims fraud screening agent
💡

No coding needed to change this. The instructions are just plain English text. Any team member can update the agent's behavior by editing the instructions — no developer required.
